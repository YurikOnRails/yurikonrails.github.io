Svaneti is what happens when a civilization builds in a place the rest of the world forgot about. The towers — defensive, medieval, still standing after a thousand years — are not monuments. People live in their shadows. Some still live inside.

The trail to Koruldi Lakes climbs through cow pastures and wildflower meadows into a landscape that belongs in a different century. At 2700m you look down on Mestia and the towers look like chess pieces on a green board.
