The F-roads of Iceland's interior are unpaved tracks through volcanic desert. No services, no phone signal, no guardrails. You carry everything — fuel, food, spare tires. The landscape is so alien it was used to train Apollo astronauts.

Landmannalaugar is the reward. Rhyolite mountains in impossible colors — pink, yellow, green, black — all in a single ridge. A natural hot spring at the base where you sit in warm water and watch the clouds move across geological time.
